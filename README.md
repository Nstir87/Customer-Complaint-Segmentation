# Customer Complaint Segmentation

## Overview

Customer complaints contain valuable insights into operational performance, service quality, and emerging business risks. However, large volumes of unstructured complaint narratives can make manual review inefficient and inconsistent.

This project applies Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to segment customer complaints into meaningful groups based on complaint content. The resulting segments help organizations identify recurring themes, prioritize investigations, and improve customer experience initiatives.

## Business Problem

Organizations often receive thousands of customer complaints through surveys, support channels, and regulatory reporting systems. Reviewing each complaint individually is time-consuming and limits an organization's ability to identify systemic issues.

By automatically grouping similar complaints, organizations can:

- Detect emerging trends
- Identify recurring customer pain points
- Improve operational processes
- Prioritize high-impact issues
- Support risk management and compliance efforts

## Dataset

The dataset consists of customer complaint narratives represented as unstructured text.

Key data elements include:

- Complaint narrative text
- Customer-reported issues
- Product or service concerns

The segmentation process successfully identified clusters of similar complaints, enabling more efficient analysis of customer feedback and operational concerns.
